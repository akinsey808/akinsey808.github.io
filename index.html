By Anabel Kinsey
In kindergarten, I started to understand that I love learning. I was learning to create and share. I had to become accustomed to others and to be kind. It was a time of discovery.
In first grade, I was exploring nature and the wonders of the world. I was spreading my borders and learning about myself. I also enjoyed learning a lot about science. This was a time of self-expansion and additions to my knowing.
In second grade, I was learning that I love to go "above" and beyond. After I established my limits and boundaries, I started exploring them. I was also getting ahead of my peers and decided to homechool the next year. Second grade was my time to look around at what I had inside of me.
Returning to Wai'alae in fourth grade, I made some steadfast friendships and had a lot of fun doing things like the Big Island Trip. I also found waysto keep myself challenged. It was a time of rebuilding my Wai'alae experience.
In fifth grade, I am nurturing my specific interests and hobbies, inspired by my school experiences. I got to go on an amazing Secret Field Trip and having fun with friends. It has been a great last year!
Hula was always important at Wai'alae. In fact, we began learning the Aloha 'Oli in the first week of school. I have improved heavily over the years and it is a very useful skill for living in Hawai'i. This skill helped me to become a well-rounded individual.
At Wai'alae 'ukulele was always known. You would make sure to keep you grades up in music so 'ukulele was easy. Then, we were always excited for the chance to be recognized for being in 'Ukulele Band. When you finally got to start playing in 4th grade we wore the correct shirts and made sure not to forget our binders. This skill helped me to be capable of multiple dimesions.
I first started loving to program when I did Hour Of Code. One hour is not enough! We had Hour of Code acople of times, all of wich I enjoyed but were unchallenging. Which is probably why I was inspired to do this website. Hour of Code helped me to become a creative problem solver.
Gardening was a huge part of my love for nature, especially the butterfly garden in kindergarten. We had 'Aina Is and garden parties. We ate the outcomes of our own hard work and watched catepillars grow in the crownflower bush. We had to work together to make it work. Gardening helped me to become collaborative.
Creativity. It isn't a subject or even an activity. It was a way of thinking. The way of letting your mind do its job. It was always subtly taught. Our teachers never told us what to do in math projects. Everything was worked with or as a side effect of creativity. This helped me to become socially responsible and self-confident.

Love, your futuristic self/ big sister you never had,

Anabel
One of the first animals I fostered were Buttons and Boots, the adorable twin kitties! They were quite the duo. On multiple occasions we would find them hanging from the tops of the kennel roof. I, of course, couldn't, for the life of me think of names. So, inanimate objects they were. I alo named our chicken Dumpling. Tsk tsk tsk. Buttons and Boots were very hard to say goodbye to.
On an especially stormy night, a family contacted us to take a stray litter of 5 kittens. We hurried on over. They were absolutely adorable. Quite a coincedence, but our beloved cat, Alana, had just passed away. So, we couldn't resist but to keep two of them, Pteradactyl and Spica. (Actually, they're on my lap as I write this.)
Our uncle, who breeds pugs, was having trouble with a mother, Chin Chin. Other than being a bad mother, she was really sweet. Yet, she would'nt pay attention to any of her nine offspring, and they all died. So, we took the next litter. She had five puggies (pug pupies), all healthy because our love and compassion (and long nights) paid off! We begged to keep one but, alas we couldn't.
Pyp. Cute, wonderful pyp. He is a tiny, yet dominant teacup chiuaua. At first sight, we could not give him back. He was barely four pounds and we were supposed o fatten him up. For two weeks. We still have him. Like I said, we just could not give him back and adopted him. Also, the spelling is because our liscense plate uses it. Love at first sight, I say!
