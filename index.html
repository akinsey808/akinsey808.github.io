Hula was always important at Wai'alae. In fact, we began learning the Aloha 'Oli in the first week of school. I have improved heavily over the years and it is a very useful skill for living in Hawai'i. This skill helped me to become a well-rounded individual.
At Wai'alae 'ukulele was always known. You would make sure to keep you grades up in music so 'ukulele was easy. Then, we were always excited for the chance to be recognized for being in 'Ukulele Band. When you finally got to start playing in 4th grade we wore the correct shirts and made sure not to forget our binders. This skill helped me to be capable of multiple dimesions.
I first started loving to program when I did Hour Of Code. One hour is not enough! We had Hour of Code acople of times, all of wich I enjoyed but were unchallenging. Which is probably why I was inspired to do this website. Hour of Code helped me to become a creative problem solver.
Gardening was a huge part of my love for nature, especially the butterfly garden in kindergarten. We had 'Aina Is and garden parties. We ate the outcomes of our own hard work and watched catepillars grow in the crownflower bush. We had to work together to make it work. Gardening helped me to become collaborative.
Creativity. It isn't a subject or even an activity. It was a way of thinking. The way of letting your mind do its job. It was always subtly taught. Our teachers never told us what to do in math projects. Everything was worked with or as a side effect of creativity. This helped me to become socially responsible and self-confident.
